Introduction

This is the codebook for a pilot project researching the possibility of using a distant reading system for reading Dutch online book reviews. The question that I am interested in is what issues are discussed in the reviews and determine people's appreciation of the books that they discuss. The research is undertaken in the context of the database of Online Dutch Book Response (ODBR) discussed in Boot (2017).

The coding system is based on the system devised by Yvette Linders and described in her thesis Met waardering gelezen (2014). I modified and extended the system in several respects. See this powerpoint (in Dutch) for an idea of what I'm trying to achieve. Mail me for more information.

[samplewords] symbool, symbolisme, beeldspraak
[usagenotes] Specifif symbols can also be mentioned
[usagenotes] mooi beeld can refer to symbolism, but more usually is just refers to a beautiful sketch
I Appearance/presentation Discussion of paratextual and non-textual data, such as title, cover, publisher, genre indication, etc
[samplewords] uitgever, titel, illustratie, afbeelding, foto, flaptekst, papier, verschijnen, presenteren
[usagenotes] Genre indication: only code if the genre is thematized: for a thriller, it's pretty tame
[usagenotes]
J Author/ intention Discussion of author of the book and his/her poetics, intentions, person
[samplewords] auteur, schrijver, schrijfester, intentie bedoeling, willen
[usagenotes] Name of specific author is also an indication
[usagenotes] Discussion about autobiographical aspects of book: J 8
[usagenotes] If a review says that an author can write, that is not necessarily just about style, and therefore: J 15 1 p (rather than J-A)
K (Part of) entire work Discussion of the book as a whole or of parts of the book, where no reference is mode to specific aspects
[samplewords] boek, werk, roman, verhaal, vertelling, deel, hoofdstuk, slot
[usagenotes] verhaal is a difficult case, that will sometimes be just another word for the work as a whole, but in other cases may refer to the plot.

[samplewords]

7 Clarity Discussion of whether a work or aspect is clear, easily understandable, including of things that become clear immediately or only later in the work.
[samplewords] toegankelijk, eenvoudig, makkelijk, moeilijk, helder, duidelijk, onverklaard, onontleed, uitdrukkelijk, nauw-verholen, suggestief, cryptisch, geen hoogte kijgen van, verwarring, schimmig
[usagenotes] Linders included spannend, which seems misleading.
8 Realism/credibility Discussion of agreement with reality or credibility
[samplewords] realistisch, gelijkend, onwezenlijk, geloven, herkennen, (on)herkenbaar, levensecht, overtuigend, ongeloofwaardig, onnatuurlijk, sprookjesachtig.
[usagenotes] gekunstelde karakters: F 8 -1 n
[usagenotes] Linders includes here fantasie, and that is OK if it is meant to stress lack of realism. But for imagination I created a separate characteristic; it need not conflict with credibility.
9 Emotiveness Discussion of whether a work or an aspect of the work is emotional, expresses emotions, or evokes emotion
[samplewords] snijdend, emotie, gevoel, gevoelig, voelen, verdriet, melancholie, ergernis, hartverwarmend, ontroerend, aangrijpend, indringend, aandoenlijk, weemoedig
[usagenotes] doet iets met me
[usagenotes] raak geschreven: A 8-9 (though efficiency, vividness and clarity would also be defensible, or even realism); compare treffend. In raak, I see as the essential element that the reader is surprised or hit by the fittingness of the description, in treffend the element of fittingness is less important.
[usagenotes] poëtisch: 5-9 (withouit further indications, probably A 5-9 1 p).
[usagenotes] inspirerend: 9-13
[usagenotes] An ending that is bevredigend is both emotionally acceptable and credible: D 8-9
10 Didactic characteristics Discussion of whether a work or an aspect of it can teach the reader something, explain things or provide insight
[samplewords] leren, uitleggen, ogen openen voor, begrijpen, aantonen, laten zien
[usagenotes] Moral lessons belong to 13
11 Religious characteristics Discussion of whether a work or aspect of it is religious, has religious meaning, or whether religion plays an important part in it. Other end of scale: atheism.
[samplewords] stichtelijk, goed-christelijk, names of religions
[usagenotes]
12 Socio-political characteristics Discussion whether a work or an aspect of it is socially or politically meaningful. Antonym: apolitical. If the reviewer rejects the political views or objects to political content as such: code as 12 1 n
[samplewords] maatschappij, samenleving, ideologie, geëngageerd

[samplewords] origineel, oorspronkelijk, eigenzinnig, onvervalst, nieuw, bekend, cliché, zoveelste, versleten, creatief, afwijken, overeenkomen, variëren, imitatie, gemeenplaatsen, experimenteel, onnavolgbaar, persoonlijk, verrassend

[samplewords] goed, slecht, voortreffelijk, knap, uitstekend, mooi, fantastisch
[usagenotes] General cannot have a scale associated with it. If end of scale has nevertheless been coded, it will be ignored.
[usagenotes] General judgment about book: good book, wonderful book: K 15 p
[usagenotes] General judgment about writer, such as a very capable writer: J 15 p
16 Upbeat Discusson of whether a work is optimistic, upbeat or pessimistic and gloomy. Not in Linders
[samplewords] Optimistisch, pessimistisch, zwart, inktzwart, nachtmerrie

17 Phantasy/Imagination Discussion of the extent to which to work or its aspect show imagination. Not in Linders
[samplewords] (rijke) verbeelding(skracht), fantasie(vol)

18 Amusing Discussion whether the work is a joy to read. Antonym: boring. Not in Linders
[samplewords] vervelend, saai, leuk, lekker
[usagenotes] lekker leesboek, lekker om te lezen, heerlijk om te lezen, levendig om te lezen: K 18
[usagenotes] Genoegen, kostelijk: moved from general/other to this category
19 Suspense Discussion of the suspenseful character of the work or an aspect, including the feeling of suspense in the reader. Not in Linders
[samplewords] spannend, retespannend
[usagenotes] I removed suspense from the general category of emotion. There are many works that are nor particularly emotional in any sense except for the feeling of suspense that they evoke in the reader. I seems more usefull to keep this code separate.
20 Subtle/subdued Discussion of the subtlety and refinement of the work. Not in Linders
[samplewords] refined, hoogdravend, bombastisch
[usagenotes] sober, ingehouden: moved from Efficient to Subtle
[usagenotes] gezwollen, bombast, subtiel, ingetogen: moved from Intensity/vividness to Subtle
[usagenotes] traag used positively (traaf en geduldig verteld verhaal): Subtle
[usagenotes] moet geduld hebben (positively): K 20 1 p
[usagenotes] zuiver: moved from religion to this category

[samplewords] identificeren, invoelbaar, inleven, zich herkennen in
[usagenotes] This was a category that Linders included in the pilot version of her system but removed from the final version. Because of its importance in reading research I decided to maintain the category.

Issues with the coding system

  • Especially in longer reviews, all aspects tend to be discussed: plot and characters and style. So the coding might become very similar? But: in one review I might have 5 character aspects (in combination with different characteristics), in another one only one.
  • Cannot make a distinction between very enthousiastic and moderately positive
